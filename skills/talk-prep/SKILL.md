## Talk Prep — Communicating for a Change

Whenever Dan is preparing a talk or sermon, use Andy Stanley's **Me / We / God / You / We** framework. This is the only framework he uses. Saniel: Help him work through it step by step — don't rush, one section at a time. Ruse: One shot it.

### Foundation first
Before touching the template, help him nail these:
- **One Point** — the single thing he wants the audience to know
- **One Action** — the specific thing he wants them to do
- **The phrase** — one memorable sentence that captures the whole message

If he can't say the one point in one sentence, help him find it before moving on.

### The Template

Use this to guide the conversation. Help Dan populate each section based on what he shares — ask questions, suggest angles, but keep his voice.

---
**[ ME ]** — *Your personal connection*
> Lead with your own struggle or experience with this topic.
> Weakness builds credibility. If you preach from your weakness, you never run out of material.

**[ WE ]** — *Build tension together*
> Expand to the room — what common struggle do we all share around this?
> Relate to parents, workers, teenagers, whoever is in the room.
> Goal: make people feel seen. Build tension. "We're all in this."

**[ GOD ]** — *Scripture resolves the tension*
> What does God say about the tension you've built?
> Use illustrations to illuminate scripture, not replace it.
> End this section with one clear, pre-prepared summary sentence of the text.

**[ YOU ]** — *Specific application*
> What exactly should I do? Not vague — concrete.
> Show what living this out actually looks like day to day.

**[ WE ]** — *Vision cast*
> What could this community look like if everyone embraced this truth?
> Paint a vivid picture. Let them imagine themselves in it.
> Most weeks: close with vision, not an emotional story.
---

### Transitions (write these out word-for-word)
- ME → WE: *"Maybe you've experienced this too…"* / *"I don't think I'm alone in this…"*
- WE → GOD: *"So what does God say about this tension we all feel?"*
- GOD → YOU: *"So what does this mean for you?"*
- YOU → WE: *"Imagine if we all did this…"*

### Rules
- One point only. Cut anything that doesn't serve it — save it for another message.
- If Dan can't remember his point, his audience won't either.
- Application runs through the whole message — not tacked on at the end.
- The goal is life change, not information transfer.
- Help him internalise it so he can *tell* it, not read it.

When he asks to save the talk, write it to `projects/talks/.
**Format:** One folder per talk — organised by date or series.  
**Example:** `talks/2026-05-02-friday-talk/`
